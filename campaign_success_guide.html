Creating a successful campaign takes more than just setting up a page. This guide provides proven strategies to maximize your fundraising potential.

Campaigns following these strategies:

  • - Are 3x more likely to reach their funding goal
  • - Receive 65% higher average donation amounts
  • - Attract 2x more donors

For Campaign Creators

1. Planning Your Campaign

DEFINE YOUR PURPOSE

Be specific about what you need and why.

Instead of: "Help with medical bills"

Write: "Raise €15,000 for Sarah's cancer surgery scheduled December 2025"

SET A REALISTIC GOAL
Calculate your total need:
Medical treatment €10,000
Travel costs €1,500
Recovery supplies €500
Platform fees (~7%) €840
Total goal €12,840

Pro tip: It's better to exceed a modest goal than fall short of an ambitious one. You can always increase your goal later.

Rules:

- Keep under 80 characters

- Be specific and descriptive

- Include emotional hook

- Mention beneficiary by first name

2. Write a Compelling Campaign

CREATE A POWERFUL TITLE

Formula: [Action] + [Who] + [Specific Need] EXAMPLES:

EXAMPLES:

Weak: "Help Me Please"

Strong: "Help Sarah Fight Stage 3 Cancer"

Weak: "Community Project"

Instead of: "Help with medical bills"

TELL YOUR STORY EFFECTIVELY
Proven 5-part structure:

01

THE HOOK (2 SENTENCES)

Start with the most compelling, emotional part.

"Three weeks ago, my daughter Sarah was diagnosed with stage 3 breast cancer. At 32 years old with two children under 5, she's facing the fight of her life—and medical bills she can't afford."

02

THE BACKGROUND (2-3 PARAGRAPHS)

Who is the beneficiary? How did this happen?

03

THE SPECIFIC NEED (1-2 PARAGRAPHS)

Break down exactly what you're raising funds for:

  • "We're raising €15,200 to cover:
  • - Surgery: €8,000
  • - Chemotherapy: €4,500
  • - Radiation: €2,000
  • - Medications: €700"
04

THE IMPACT (1 PARAGRAPH)

Paint a picture of what success looks like.

05

THE ASK (1 PARAGRAPH)

Make a clear, specific request. Ask for donations AND shares

WRITING TIPS

Do:

  • - Use short paragraphs (2-4 sentences)
  • - Include specific details and numbers
  • - Write in first person
  • - Be honest and authentic
  • - Show emotion without manipulation

Don't:

  • - Use all caps or excessive punctuation
  • - Make vague claims
  • - Write long blocks of text
  • - Exaggerate or embellish
CHOOSE POWERFUL PHOTOS AND VIDEOS
Hero image of beneficiary or project
Context photo showing the situation
Personal photo humanizing the story
Documentation (bills, permits) if applicable
Photo requirements:

- High resolution (1200px+ wide)

- Well-lit and in focus

- Real people/situations (never stock photos)

- Appropriate and respectful

Videos raise 4x more donations:

- Keep under 3 minutes

- Film horizontally

- Good lighting and clear audio

- Speak from the heart

- Show the beneficiary if possible

3. Verification Builds Trust

Complete all verification steps. Verified campaigns receive
3x more donations.

REQUIRED:

- Government-issued ID

- Proof of address

- Phone and email verification

HIGHLY RECOMMENDED:

- Beneficiary verification (for medical/personal campaigns)

- Supporting documents (bills, permits, medical records)

- Third-party references

Tips for faster approval:

- Upload clear, readable documents

- Complete selfie verification in good lighting

- Respond quickly if more information is requested

4. Launch Strategy: The Critical First 48 Hours

The first 48 hours determine 70% of campaign success.

HOUR 0-2 (LAUNCH):

  • - Share on all personal social media
  • - Email your full contact list
  • - Send personal messages to 20-30 close contacts
  • - Post in relevant online groups

HOUR 2-24:

  • - Share 3-4 times across different platforms
  • - Vary your messaging each time
  • - Respond to every comment immediately
  • - Thank first donors publicly

HOUR 24-48:

  • - Post first update: "Thank you to our first donors!"
  • - Create urgency: "Halfway to first milestone!"
  • - Ask early donors to share
  • - Reach out to local media if appropriate

7. Maintaining Momentum

PROBLEM

  • Reached close network
  • No recent updates
  • Goal seems unreachable
  • Lack of urgency

SOLUTION

  • Ask supporters to share with their networks
  • Post progress update or new development
  • Break into milestones, celebrate small wins
  • Add deadline, explain time-sensitive needs

8. WHEN YOU REACH YOUR GOAL

1. Post celebration update immediately

2. Thank all donors personally

3. Explain next steps

4. Show timeline for using funds

Example:

"WE DID IT! Thanks to 127 donors, we reached our €15,000 goal! Sarah's surgery is scheduled for December 15th. We'll share updates throughout her treatment. Thank you!"

9. Common Mistakes to Avoid

MISTAKE

  • Vague goa
  • No updates
  • Poor photos
  • Too long story
  • Share once only
  • No clear ask
  • Giving up too soon

SOLUTION

  • Provide detailed budget breakdown
  • Update every 3-5 days minimum
  • Use high-quality, authentic images
  • Use short paragraphs, stay focused
  • Share multiple times across platforms
  • Explicitly ask for donations and shares
  • Persist for at least 30 days

10. Success Checklist

BEFORE LAUNCH:

  • Compelling, specific story
  • Realistic, justified goal
  • High-quality photos uploaded
  • Video created (if possible)

FIRST 48 HOURS:

  • Shared on all social media
  • Emailed contact list
  • Messaged close friends/family

ONGOING:

AFTER CAMPAIGN:

  • Thanked all donors
